Color Grading Parameters

Exposure

Adjusts overall brightness. Range -1 to +1. Affects all pixels uniformly across the image.

Contrast

Controls difference between light and dark areas. Increases depth and visual separation.

Saturation

Intensity of colors. -1 removes all color (grayscale), +1 maximizes color vibrancy.

Temperature

Shifts color toward warm (orange) or cool (blue). Simulates different lighting conditions.

Tint

Adjusts magenta/green balance. Fine-tunes color cast for accurate skin tones.

Highlights

Targets bright pixels (luminance >180). Recover blown-out areas or enhance brightness.

Shadows

Targets dark pixels (luminance <75). Lift shadows or deepen blacks for mood.

Cinematic Presets

Cinematic

Subtle warm tones with lifted shadows and enhanced contrast. Classic film look for dramas.

Noir

High contrast black and white with crushed shadows. Perfect for mystery and crime genres.

Vintage

Desaturated warm tones with lifted blacks. Nostalgic 1970s aesthetic.

Bleach Bypass

Reduced contrast with boosted saturation and highlights. Modern action film aesthetic.

Warm

Strong orange temperature shift with enhanced saturation. Sunset and golden hour feel.

None (Reset)

Resets all parameters to 0. Returns to original ungraded footage.

Professional Video Scopes

Waveform Monitor

Displays luminance values across the horizontal axis of the image. Vertical position represents brightness (0-255). Use to check exposure balance and ensure highlights/shadows stay within broadcast-safe ranges.

Vectorscope

Shows color distribution in UV color space. Center represents neutral gray, distance from center indicates saturation, angle represents hue. Use to verify accurate skin tones and identify color casts.

RGB Histogram

Three overlaid histograms (red, green, blue) showing pixel distribution across tonal ranges. Left side is shadows, right side is highlights. Use to identify clipping and ensure full tonal range utilization.
