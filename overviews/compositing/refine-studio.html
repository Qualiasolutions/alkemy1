What is Refine Studio?

Refine Studio is an advanced image refinement interface that allows you to perfect AI-generated images through iterative prompt-based editing. Adjust lighting, modify composition, add atmospheric effects, and fine-tune every visual element until your shot is perfect.

Refinement Workflow

1
Select Image for Refinement
Choose any generated image from Scene Assembler, Moodboard, or Character Generator to enter Refine Studio.
2
Analyze Current Image
Review the image and identify areas for improvement: lighting, composition, atmosphere, details, or character expression.
3
Craft Refinement Prompt
Write specific instructions: "Make lighting warmer," "Add cinematic fog," "Enhance facial expression," "Adjust composition rule of thirds."
4
Generate Refinement
AI applies changes while preserving character identity and core composition. Preview results in real-time.
5
Compare & Select
Side-by-side comparison of original vs refined image. Accept, retry with different prompt, or continue iterating.
6
Save & Apply
Save refined version as main image or additional variant. Automatically updates Scene Assembler and Presentation.

Refinement Categories

Lighting & Atmosphere

Adjust light temperature, intensity, direction. Add atmospheric effects: fog, haze, volumetric lighting, god rays.

Color & Tone

Modify color palette, saturation, contrast. Apply cinematic looks, adjust white balance, create mood variations.

Composition & Framing

Reposition elements, adjust camera angles, apply rule of thirds, modify depth of field, change perspective.

Character & Expression

Fine-tune facial expressions, posture, emotions. Maintain identity while adjusting mood and performance.

Environment & Details

Add or modify background elements, enhance textures, improve details, add props, adjust weather conditions.

Style & Art Direction

Apply artistic styles, adjust realism level, add painterly effects, change genre, match reference styles.

Advanced Techniques

Progressive Refinement Strategy

  • Coarse to Fine: Start with major adjustments, then progressively fine-tune details
  • Layered Approach: Refine lighting first, then atmosphere, then details, finally characters
  • Branching Exploration: Create multiple variations at key decision points
  • Reference Matching: Use moodboard images as style guides for consistent aesthetic

Professional Prompting Examples

  • Lighting: "Add warm golden hour lighting from the left, soft shadows, subtle lens flare"
  • Atmosphere: "Add light cinematic fog in the background, volumetric rays through windows"
  • Composition: "Reposition subject using rule of thirds, increase depth of field"
  • Character: "Enhance facial expression to show determination, maintain identity"
